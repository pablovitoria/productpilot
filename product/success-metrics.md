# Success Metrics

Success will therefore be measured not only by efficiency, but also by the quality of the product discovery process and the confidence of the Product Manager.

#### Product Objective

Help Product Managers make better product decisions faster while preserving human judgment.

## MVP Success Criteria

The MVP will be considered successful if Product Managers can:

- Move from raw customer feedback to a validated product decision in under 10 minutes.
- Feel confident that the final output reflects their own judgment rather than AI-generated recommendations.
- Combine qualitative customer insights with quantitative product data before making a decision.
- Generate reusable product artifacts that support product discussions with engineering, design, and business stakeholders.

---

# North Star Metric

## Decision Cycle Time

**Definition**

The average time required to move from unstructured customer feedback to a validated product decision.

**Why it matters**

ProductPilot exists to reduce the time spent organizing information while preserving thoughtful product judgment.

---

# Product OKRs

## Objective

Enable faster, more structured product discovery without sacrificing decision quality.

### Key Results

- Reduce average product discovery time by at least **50%**.
- 80% of users report increased confidence in their final product decision.
- 70% of users complete the full ProductPilot workflow.
- 80% of generated outputs are used as the basis for product discussions with minimal edits.

# Product KPIs

These metrics measure the overall health and effectiveness of ProductPilot.

### Efficiency

- Decision Cycle Time
- Average Time per Workflow
- Workflow Completion Rate

### Adoption

- Active Users
- Repeat Usage Rate
- Output Type Distribution (Decision Canvas, Epic Draft, PRD Draft)

### User Satisfaction

After completing a workflow, users are asked:

> **Did ProductPilot help you make a better product decision?**

Rating:

- 1 – Strongly Disagree
- 2 – Disagree
- 3 – Neutral
- 4 – Agree
- 5 – Strongly Agree

---

# Feature Metrics

These metrics evaluate whether individual ProductPilot features are providing value.

## Supporting Product Data

- Percentage of workflows including supporting product metrics.
- Average number of supporting metrics added per workflow.

## AI Challenge

- Percentage of AI challenges accepted.
- Percentage of AI challenges resulting in changes to the final decision.
- Percentage of AI challenges dismissed by the Product Manager.

## Opportunity Selection

- Average number of opportunities reviewed.
- Distribution of selected opportunities.
- Percentage of first-ranked opportunities selected.

## Evidence Coverage

**Definition**

The percentage of final decisions supported by both qualitative customer evidence and quantitative product data.

This metric reinforces ProductPilot's philosophy that great product decisions combine customer insight with measurable business evidence.

---

# MVP Assumptions

The MVP assumes that:

- Guided workflows produce better product decisions than open-ended prompting.
- AI is most valuable when generating and organizing hypotheses rather than making decisions.
- Product Managers benefit from combining qualitative customer insights with quantitative product data.
- Challenging assumptions leads to higher-quality product decisions.
- Structured decision-making produces more useful product artifacts than generating documents from a blank prompt.

These assumptions should be validated through future user testing.
