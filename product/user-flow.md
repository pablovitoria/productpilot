# User Flow

## Overview

ProductPilot is designed around a structured product discovery workflow.

Rather than generating product documents directly, ProductPilot guides Product Managers through a repeatable decision-making process that combines AI-generated insights with customer evidence, business context, product data, and human judgment.

The goal is not to automate product thinking, but to help Product Managers make better product decisions, faster.

## Step 1 — Customer Feedback

The workflow begins with unstructured customer feedback.

Examples include:

- Customer interviews
- App Store reviews
- Support tickets
- Survey responses
- Sales conversations
- Reddit discussions
- Internal notes

The Product Manager simply pastes the feedback into ProductPilot.

---

## Step 2 — AI Analysis

AI performs the first-pass synthesis of the feedback.

The objective is to reduce the time spent organizing information—not making decisions.

The analysis includes:

- Common themes
- Pain points
- Customer sentiment
- Representative quotes
- Recurring requests
- Emerging patterns

---

## Step 3 — Potential Opportunity Identification

Based on the synthesized feedback, ProductPilot proposes several potential product opportunities.

Rather than recommending a single solution, the AI presents multiple opportunities for consideration.

Example:

1. Improve Booking Search
2. Simplify Checkout Flow
3. Introduce Flexible Payments
4. Improve Group Chat
5. Enhance Trip Discovery

The Product Manager selects the opportunity they want to evaluate further.

At this stage, these are only hypotheses—not product decisions.

---

## Step 4 — PM Decision Workspace

This is the core experience of ProductPilot.

Rather than asking the Product Manager to simply approve AI suggestions, ProductPilot guides them through a structured evaluation process.

### 1. Validate the Opportunity

Questions include:

- Is this a real customer problem?
- Does the available evidence support it?
- Should additional research be completed first?

---

### 2. Customer Evidence

AI provides an editable draft containing:

- Themes
- Customer quotes
- Pain points

The Product Manager can edit, remove, or expand the evidence.

---

### 3. Supporting Product Data

The Product Manager enriches qualitative feedback with quantitative evidence.

Examples include:

- Feature adoption
- Conversion rate
- Retention
- Revenue impact
- Support volume
- Customer segments
- NPS
- Any relevant KPI

This ensures product decisions combine customer insight with measurable business data.

---

### 4. Business Context

The Product Manager answers structured questions such as:

- Which business objective does this support?
- Why is this important now?
- Which users are affected?
- What happens if this problem is not solved?

---

### 5. Opportunity Assessment

The Product Manager evaluates:

- Customer impact
- Business impact
- Strategic alignment
- Confidence
- Estimated effort (optional)

The purpose is not prioritization, but structured decision-making.

---

## Step 5 — AI Challenge

Before generating any output, ProductPilot challenges the Product Manager's assumptions.

Rather than validating every decision, AI acts as a thinking partner.

Examples include:

> Most evidence comes from highly engaged users. Could casual users experience a different problem?

> Feature adoption suggests users rarely reach this step. Could the root cause occur earlier in the journey?

> You selected Retention as the primary objective. Would reducing booking abandonment better align with the available evidence?

The Product Manager may revise their evaluation before continuing.

---

## Step 6 — Output Selection

Once the opportunity has been fully evaluated, the Product Manager chooses which artifact to generate.

Available outputs include:

- Product Decision Canvas
- Epic Draft
- PRD Draft

Each output is generated using:

- AI synthesis
- Customer evidence
- Product metrics
- Business context
- PM judgment
- AI challenge responses

The generated artifact represents the Product Manager's final decision—not the AI's recommendation.

# Design Principles

ProductPilot is built around five principles.

## AI accelerates thinking.

Not decision-making.

---

## Humans own product judgment.

AI proposes.

Product Managers decide.

---

## Evidence over opinions.

Every recommendation should connect back to customer evidence and product data.

---

## Structured thinking beats blank pages.

Guided workflows produce better decisions than open-ended prompting.

---

## Better questions lead to better products.

ProductPilot exists to help Product Managers ask better questions before building solutions.
